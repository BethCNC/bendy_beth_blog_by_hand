# Bendy BethC Website - Purpose Summary

## Overview

The **Bendy BethC** website is a chronic illness advocacy platform that serves as the digital expansion of Beth's Instagram presence. This website is more than a blog or portfolio—it's a reclaiming of narrative, a beacon for others in the chronic illness community, and a polished example of Beth's skill as a multidisciplinary designer.

## Core Mission

**Transforming chronic illness advocacy through authentic storytelling, medical literacy, and community building** within the EDS/MCAS/POTS and neurodivergent communities.

The website bridges the gap between medical complexity and human experience by documenting, educating, and advocating for those living with:
- **Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome (EDS)**
- **Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS)**
- **Postural Orthostatic Tachycardia Syndrome (POTS)**
- **Late-diagnosed autism**

## Brand Position

**"The Medical Storyteller"** — A trusted voice in chronic illness advocacy who transforms personal health documentation into powerful educational content, making invisible illnesses visible through authentic narrative and medical literacy.

## Primary Purpose

The website serves as:

1. **A Narrative Reclamation** — Taking back control of the story around chronic illness, moving beyond medical gaslighting and dismissal
2. **An Educational Resource** — Providing meticulously researched content that balances clinical accuracy with accessibility
3. **A Community Hub** — Creating a space where medical complexity meets human compassion
4. **An Advocacy Tool** — Empowering others through documentation systems, medical literacy, and practical guidance
5. **A Professional Showcase** — Demonstrating Beth's multidisciplinary design skills while serving a greater purpose

## Target Audiences

### Primary: The Seeking Warrior (25-45)
- Recently diagnosed or undiagnosed individuals experiencing complex symptoms
- Overwhelmed by medical systems and seeking guidance
- Craves validation and practical advocacy tools
- Values authenticity over inspiration porn
- Neurodivergent-friendly communication preferences

### Secondary: The Veteran Advocate (30-55)
- Established in their chronic illness journey
- Seeks community and advanced advocacy strategies
- Interested in medical research and documentation methods
- Potential collaborators and content amplifiers

### Tertiary: The Learning Ally (Any age)
- Healthcare providers, family members, friends
- Seeking education about invisible illnesses
- Wanting to better support chronically ill individuals

## Content Strategy

### Core Content Pillars

1. **Medical Documentation & Advocacy (30%)**
   - Health binder organization and systems
   - Medical appointment preparation guides
   - Insurance and disability navigation
   - Research literacy and medical terminology

2. **Personal Journey Storytelling (40%)**
   - Daily life with multiple chronic conditions
   - Medical appointment experiences and outcomes
   - Emotional and mental health aspects
   - Milestone moments and setbacks

3. **Community Education & Awareness (20%)**
   - EDS/MCAS/POTS symptom explanations
   - Neurodivergent-chronic illness intersections
   - Accessibility and accommodation strategies
   - Medical system navigation tips

4. **Community Building & Support (10%)**
   - Highlighting follower stories and experiences
   - Q&A sessions and live discussions
   - Resource sharing and recommendations
   - Collaboration with other advocates

## Visual & Design Philosophy

### Design Principles
- **Softness, Openness, and Clarity** — Balancing heartfelt storytelling with empowerment and professionalism
- **Neurodivergent-Friendly** — High contrast ratios, accessible typography, clear navigation
- **Warm but Professional** — Never sterile or clinical, but maintaining credibility
- **Rainbow Palette** — Reflects neurodivergent-friendly design and diverse experiences

### Typography
- **DM Sans (Sans-serif):** Clean, accessible primary text
- **Behind The Nineties (Serif):** Warmth and personality for headers/accents

### Visual Metaphors
- **The Health Binder:** Central visual metaphor for organization and advocacy
- **Documented Journey:** Charts, timelines, and progress tracking
- **Invisible Made Visible:** Subtle textures and layers
- **Stretchiness/Bendiness:** Flexible shapes and gentle curves
- **Community Threads:** Connecting lines and interwoven elements
